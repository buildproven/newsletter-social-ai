## Why It's Different

This is not another tool that generates bad AI content.

The prompts are engineered from real performance data — 128,000+ LinkedIn impressions analyzed to find exactly what separates posts that hit 24,000 impressions from posts that hit 129. The difference comes down to one thing: the first line.

The AI knows:
- Why "I ran Claude Code for 40 days straight. Here's the honest scorecard." → 24,599 impressions
- Why "I've watched enough AI product builds go sideways..." → 129 impressions
- Why concrete numbers always outperform vague claims
- Why "Builder Trap" posts (naming a specific mistake) consistently outperform generic advice
- Why honest admissions spread further than positive spin

That intelligence is baked into the prompt. The app won't generate posts that open with "I'm excited to share" or dump 15 hashtags at the bottom. Those patterns are explicitly ruled out because the data says they don't work.

**The result:** posts that skew toward what actually performs, not what sounds like AI output.
